Q4 (CAF foundational capability) — picked C Governance. CAF perspectives are People, Business, Governance, Platform, Security, Operations. The others (Sustainability, Performance efficiency, Reliability) are Well-Architected pillars, which is the common distractor here.
Q113 (CAF Business perspective) — picked A Program and project management. The Business perspective includes Strategy Management, Portfolio Management, Innovation, Product Management, Strategic Partnership, Data Monetization, Business Insights, Data Science, Program/Project Management etc. I went with A as the most clearly Business-perspective answer.
Q150 (provide credential list to auditor) — picked C IAM credential report. The simplest single-click download.
Q156 (review S3 buckets with ACLs/policies in S3 console) — picked D Access Analyzer for S3. The S3-console-integrated view of who has external access. B (S3 Storage Lens) is the runner-up; Storage Lens is about usage analytics, not ACL/policy review.
Q186 (cloud transformation phase for measurable business outcomes) — picked A Envision. The AWS CAF transformation domains/phases (Envision → Align → Launch → Scale) put business outcome identification in Envision.
Q207 (CAF perspective: digitally fluent workforce) — picked B People. CAF People perspective covers Culture, Transformational Leadership, Cloud Fluency, Workforce Transformation, Change Management, Organization Design, Organizational Alignment.
Q230 (customer responsibilities, Choose two) — picked [C, D] (guest OS config + encryption decisions). [C, A] could also be argued if "configuration of infrastructure devices" is read as customer-side gear, but in AWS shared responsibility wording, infrastructure devices = AWS-side.
